About Me
UI/UX Designer
I am an American Graphic Designer living in San Diego, California.

I am currently pursuing a BS Design degree as a senior at Biola University. At an early age I developed a passion for the arts which led me into diving into the digital world.

Previous Experience

In Second Grade, I realized one day that I had a talent for drawing. When I knew I could draw something just by looking at it, I spent most of my time teaching myself how to do it better and more efficiently. I remember being so eager when I got home from school to get working on the next project. The satisfaction that came with completing something and seeing both of my parents in amazement is what made me realize that I wanted to do this full time. The process of creating art is a wonderful thing, but knowing that it brings others joy is the most rewarding feeling.

In middle school, I taught myself how to blend with graphite and began creating my own techniques to achieve realism in my projects. Hyperrealism became something I was fascinated with and tried to add as much detail to my pieces as possible.

Drawing soon became the healthiest habit in my life, but it also became one of the only things that got me through the darkest time. I battled anxiety for almost five years, and the practice of creating art gave me strength to keep going.

In my high school years, I took AP Studio Art for three years, yet quickly became burnt out with drawing as a whole. During this time, I transferred mediums from traditional graphite to colored pencil and became obsessed with highly saturated hues and double exposure techniques.

From paper to Pixel

Over time I wanted to layout my designs in some sort of digital space, so I began teaching myself how to use Photoshop. I ended up working with Affinity Photo and Designer by Serif Labs because of their one time payment and near-identical features. The digital workspace became a familiar place for me to create compositions that I would draw for my Studio Art portfolios. I worked on getting experience with the Adobe Suite in high school, and during the process I became interested in Graphic Design. I began creating logos and thumbnails for a small YouTube channel I had at the time to document some of my work. Photo manipulation became one of my favorite things to do, and for the longest time I wanted to learn the ins and outs of Photoshop. ‍

Experience in the digital world led me to a passion for apparel design, logos, and web development to name a few. I grew to love creating layouts and working with typography as a way to create unique interfaces.

I currently work as the Graphic Designer for Chino Valley Community Church and plan on graduating from Biola University in Fall of 2022.
